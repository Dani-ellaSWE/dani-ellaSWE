What Mental Health Is

Mental health is how we think, feel, and cope with everyday life. It includes our emotions, stress levels, confidence, relationships, and how we deal with challenges.

Everyone has mental health, just like everyone has physical health. It can go up and down depending on what is happening in our lives. Having mental health struggles does not mean someone is weak—it means they may need support.

How It Can Affect People

Mental health can affect sleep, energy, motivation, concentration, and behaviour. When someone is struggling, everyday things like school, work, or talking to people can feel much harder than usual.

What Can Cause Mental Health Struggles

Mental health struggles usually happen because of a mix of reasons. This can include stress, difficult life events, bullying, trauma, physical health problems, sleep issues, hormones, genetics (family history), and the environment around us (feeling unsafe, unsupported, or isolated).

Sometimes there is one clear cause, and other times it builds up slowly. Either way, mental health is real health.
