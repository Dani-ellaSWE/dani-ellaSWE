Urgent Help

If you or someone else is in immediate danger, call 999. If you need urgent mental health support, you can contact your local NHS urgent mental health helpline via the NHS website or call 111 and select the mental health option.

24/7 Helplines (UK)

  • Samaritans: 116 123 – Free and available 24/7
  • Shout: Text SHOUT to 85258 – Free text support
  • Childline: 0800 1111 – For under 19s
  • YoungMinds: Text YM to 85258
  • Mind: 0300 123 3393
  • NHS Mental Health Services

Self-Help Strategies

  • Follow a daily routine to add structure
  • Practice breathing exercises for 5 minutes
  • Limit social media if it affects your mood
  • Stay connected with at least one trusted person
  • Write down three small things you achieved today
  • Try guided meditation videos on YouTube

Understanding Mental Health

Mental health affects how we think, feel, and behave. It can change due to stress, life events, trauma, or biological factors. Seeking support early can make a significant difference.

If symptoms last more than two weeks or interfere with daily life, it may be helpful to speak to a GP. GPs can refer individuals to counselling or NHS therapy services such as IAPT (Improving Access to Psychological Therapies).
