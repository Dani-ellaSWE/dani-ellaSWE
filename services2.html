This page includes support ideas for mental health and healthcare, coping strategies for common struggles, and trusted links where you can get help. If you or someone else is in danger, use the urgent support section below.

Mental Health Support Services

These are examples of support you can explore. You can use them alongside professional help.

Talking Support

  • Speaking with a trusted adult, mentor, teacher, or friend
  • School or college wellbeing teams
  • Counselling and therapy (online or in-person)
  • Support groups for anxiety, depression, grief, or stress

Self-Help and Wellbeing

  • Journaling and mood tracking
  • Mindfulness and breathing exercises
  • Healthy sleep routines
  • Movement (walks, stretching, dancing, sports)
  • Creative outlets (art, music, writing)

Crisis and Immediate Support

  • If you feel unsafe, contact emergency services
  • Text or call crisis support services (see links below)
  • Go to A&E if you need urgent medical help

Support for Different Needs

  • Support for anxiety and panic
  • Support for low mood and depression
  • Support for stress, burnout, and exam pressure
  • Support for grief and loss
  • Support for neurodiversity (ADHD, autism) wellbeing

Healthcare Support Services

Healthcare support can include physical health, mental health, and lifestyle support. If you are not sure where to start, a GP (doctor) is a good first step.

GP and Primary Care

  • Book a GP appointment to discuss symptoms and support options
  • Ask about referrals (talking therapies, mental health teams, specialist care)
  • Medication advice and reviews (if relevant)

Healthy Habits Support

  • Sleep support and routines
  • Nutrition and hydration tips
  • Exercise and activity plans that feel manageable
  • Support for long-term health conditions

Appointments and Advocacy

  • Write down symptoms and questions before appointments
  • Bring a trusted person if you feel nervous
  • Ask for explanations in simple terms if needed

When to Seek Urgent Medical Help

  • Severe symptoms or sudden worsening
  • Chest pain, breathing problems, fainting, serious injury
  • Emergency mental health risk (see urgent support below)

Coping With Mental Health Struggles

These coping ideas are simple and realistic. Not everything works for everyone, so try a few and keep what helps.

When You Feel Overwhelmed

  • Try box breathing: breathe in 4, hold 4, out 4, hold 4 (repeat)
  • Use the 5-4-3-2-1 grounding method (senses)
  • Drink water and sit somewhere safe for a moment
  • Break tasks into the next smallest step

When You Feel Anxious

  • Write down the worry, then write one helpful response to it
  • Do a short walk or stretch to release tension
  • Limit caffeine if it makes symptoms worse
  • Talk to someone you trust

When You Feel Low

  • Do one small thing: shower, tidy one corner, or eat something
  • Try a “low energy plan” (simple meals, rest, gentle movement)
  • Spend a few minutes outside if possible
  • Reach out for support early, not only in crisis

Daily Habits That Can Help

  • Regular sleep routine (same wake-up time if you can)
  • Plan breaks during the day
  • Reduce doom-scrolling when you feel worse
  • Make a short list of calming activities

Get Help and Support Links

If you are in immediate danger or you feel you might harm yourself or someone else, call your local emergency number right away. If you are in the UK, call 999 or go to A&E.

If you are not in immediate danger but need urgent support, you can contact a crisis service or NHS support.

  • Shout (UK text support)
  • YoungMinds
  • NHS Mental Health
  • NHS: Find a GP
  • NHS: Urgent mental health helplines
  • Samaritans
  • Mind

If you are outside the UK, you can search for local crisis numbers or national mental health helplines in your country.
